# Immersive VFX/HUD feedback under mobile readability limits

## Research question and scope

How should Abyssal Command present basic automatic attacks, skills, critical hits, health, cooldowns, boss arrival, and stage entry so that the player can read threat and build outcomes at mobile scale without letting visual spectacle claim gameplay authority?

This packet covers presentation only. It does not alter defense-catalog rules, targeting, movement, combat results, economy, audio implementation, or narrative. **Source-derived** labels a fact from the ledger. **Target** labels an original, falsifiable acceptance threshold rather than an external fact. “Effect” means a visible projection; it never means a rule-side particle, collision, timer, or damage source.

## Product constraints

- The deterministic 60 Hz simulation and `defense-catalog.js` remain the only rules authority. Canvas/HUD/VFX/audio/narration observe confirmed snapshots/events; they may not advance a timer, choose a target, create damage, consume a cooldown, change RNG, or block movement.
- Automatic combat and movement-first input stay intact. HUD information does not create attack, aim, lock-on, or tactical interactions.
- The product is offline and local-first. This proposal has no network, account, commerce, or gameplay-time service dependency.
- Canon remains W-01 Command Echo, W-02 Bind/Extract, W-03 Gate Integrity, W-04 Domain, and W-05 Archive. Stage 10 is the ending; a stage-entry treatment must not imply an eleventh stage or add canon not authored elsewhere.
- Reduced motion is an equal semantic projection, not a feature that hides meaningful state.

### Source-quality and boundary notes

S1–S6 are primary/official sources; S7 is specialist industry guidance. The sources do **not** prove a particular attack duration, particle count, engagement outcome, or performance budget. Those are deliberately marked **Target** below. No external title’s assets, names, HUD layout, effects, or narrative are copied.

## Observed patterns and synthesis

1. **Priority is meaning before intensity.** S1 requires essential information to survive loss of color, sound, or haptics. Therefore a threatening boss arrival, a usable skill, and a critical result need distinct semantic shapes/labels; simply making all three brighter creates competition rather than hierarchy.
2. **Persistent state and transient causality belong in different places.** S2 explicitly calls out health meters/directional cues. Stable self-state belongs in an edge HUD; the reason something happened belongs at its world origin or the affected actor. Floating health bars for every common enemy would turn transient detail into constant noise.
3. **Motion is decoration unless it carries unavailable state.** S5 permits removing nonessential movement. A sweep can make a skill feel weighty, but a static boundary/icon must still tell the player where it resolved and whether it is cooling down.
4. **High-impact spectacle has a safety cost.** S3/S4 make flashes, saturated red transitions, and large bright areas especially risky. The answer is one controlled emphasis at a time—not a warning screen, not a settings-only cure after a first unsafe event.
5. **Mobile control affordance is separate from combat readability.** S6 applies only to items that can be tapped. A passive health bar need not be enlarged into a touch target; a pause/accessibility control must be. This preserves the movement-first playfield.

## Original feedback language: the Abyssal feedback stack

The following is an original proposal.

| Rank | Stack layer | What it may communicate | Render rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 — rule observation | Confirmed snapshot/event adapter | Entity ID, simulation tick, event kind, value/state, world position | The adapter is read-only and event IDs are `(tick, entityId, kind, ordinal)`. A missing presentation event cannot change simulation. |
| 1 — survival signal | World boundary / directional marker and stable W-03 HUD | Imminent or confirmed player harm, Gate danger, boss active state | Reserved above all flourish. Lower ranks may not cover its boundary, recolor it, or draw a particle over its center. |
| 2 — command result | World-local skill shape, critical glyph, skill cooldown state | A confirmed automatic action or a player-available ability state | Uses shape + icon/text + color; state remains legible with motion/audio/haptics off. |
| 3 — progress identity | Selected elite/boss bar, W-01/W-02/W-04/W-05 motif | Persistent target identity or authored system identity | One target detail set at a time; common units get no persistent bar. |
| 4 — texture | Normal impacts, residue, ambient drift, optional hit audio/haptic | Nonessential combat texture | First layer to aggregate, cull, shorten, or mute under density pressure. Never announces a rule by itself. |

### Density governor

The presentation adapter assigns every visible request a rank, footprint mask, start/end tick, and optional static equivalent. It enforces these original **Targets** at each rendered frame:

- **Target:** at most one rank-1 full-screen-adjacent treatment (boss warning or stage label) at a time; it must leave the avatar and active hostile boundaries unobscured.
- **Target:** rank-4 translucent coverage may occupy at most 18% of the playable viewport at alpha >= 0.15, and at most 8% inside a 160dp radius around an active rank-1 boundary. The capture checker computes this from layer masks, not visual judgment.
- **Target:** no more than six rank-4 emitters and two floating aggregate labels per 500 ms within a 160dp player-radius; overflow coalesces into one local mark or is culled oldest-first.
- **Target:** rank-2/3 effects may coexist with rank-1 only when their masks do not intersect the rank-1 protected zone. A critical hit loses its flourish before a danger cue loses contrast.
- **Target:** event order in the presentation queue is stable for a given simulation replay. Culling uses a deterministic sort key only for capture reproducibility; the emitted visual result does not feed back to simulation.

## Technical asset and capture requirements

### Asset contract

1. **Semantic manifest.** Every VFX/HUD asset declares `eventKind`, rank, world/HUD anchor, protected-zone behavior, max lifetime in ticks, reduced-motion asset/state, color-independent shape/icon, and localizable text key. An asset without a static equivalent cannot be assigned to rank 1–3.
2. **Bounds are authored separately from rules.** Skill VFX must receive an observed resolved area/target path and clip its art to it where feasible. A visual boundary is never used as a collision or range source. A mismatch is a presentation defect, not an invitation to change a rule.
3. **Contrast-safe variants.** Important glyphs/bars ship with adaptive light/dark outline and high-contrast variants; the capture harness samples contrast against the worst pixel in their rendered backing. S2’s 4.5:1/3:1/7:1 values are the source-derived floors used by the test.
4. **No implicit animation loop.** Assets must have declared start/end frames and cannot loop except an explicitly approved passive gauge fill. Continuous drift, pulsing, or flashing is rejected for status state.
5. **Atlas and draw hygiene.** Each event declares sprite count and subparticle maximum from the event table. A capture has a per-event draw/event log; unbounded allocation, random visual seeds, and runtime-loaded art are prohibited. Cosmetic variation is seeded from the observed event ID, not wall-clock time.
6. **Accessible text/control semantics.** Any actual HUD button has an accessible label and source-derived 48dp target (S6). Decorative canvas art is not exposed as a fake control. Passive vital/cooldown state needs an equivalent settings/narration description where that product surface exists; this report does not claim full screen-reader support for the battlefield.

### Capture matrix and gates

Use fixed simulation snapshots and input replays for all captures. Capture 60 fps output at two fixed logical landscape extremes: **Target low:** 480×270 CSS pixels at 1× render scale (480×270 backing store); **Target high/baseline v1:** 844×390 CSS pixels at 3× render scale (2532×1170 backing store). The capture manifest records CSS size, backing-store size, device pixel ratio, render scale, zoom, and safe-area insets; any other size is supplemental rather than a replacement. Each extreme runs high-contrast, reduced-motion, effects-muted, and a color-vision simulation **prefilter**. The XAG 103 source explicitly says simulation does not replace testing with players with color vision deficiency. The performance profile is fixed before a gate run: **Target baseline v1:** one physical iPhone 12, current supported iOS and production browser recorded in the manifest, high-viewport mode, 60 Hz display mode, battery above 80%, Low Power Mode off, network disabled after the local build loads, and a 3-minute warm-up replay. A result without this manifest is invalid, not “rebased.”

## Implications for Abyssal Command

1. **DI-1 — Ship hierarchy as a renderer contract, not a style suggestion.** Rank and protected-zone metadata must accompany every presentation event. **Target:** the dense deterministic fixture produces zero rank-4-over-rank-1 mask violations.
2. **DI-2 — Make crits and skills semantically distinct without escalation.** The fracture ring and ability geometry must distinguish event classes in static vision, rather than relying on brightness, red, shake, or larger particle counts. **Target:** each event-table recognition test meets its visual-only score with audio/haptics disabled.
3. **DI-3 — Treat health/cooldown as calm, inspectable instruments.** Player and Gate status persist on the edge; only an elite/boss earns persistent target detail. **Target:** no common-enemy persistent bar in the high-density capture and health/cooldown recognition gates pass in a single static frame.
4. **DI-4 — Reduced motion is the production baseline for semantics.** Make the static asset/state first and layer standard motion above it. **Target:** the reduced-motion capture preserves event identity, location/area, phase, health, and readiness at the same recognition threshold as standard mode (within five percentage points).
5. **DI-5 — Use capture evidence to cap spectacle.** The governor culls texture before it compromises a threat, boss location, or vital HUD. **Target:** all high-density fixtures pass mask, flash, performance, and replay-invariance gates before effect count is increased.

## Risks, contradictions, and safeguards

- **Authority-leak failure mode.** A VFX could begin from predicted attack intent, then be read by the player as a confirmed hit/area even if rules reject it. **Test:** inject rejected/missed/retargeted intents into a replay and assert no confirmed-hit, crit, health, cooldown-ready, or boss-arrival presentation fires until its confirmed observed event/state exists. Verify simulation hashes unchanged if the entire presentation adapter is disabled.
- **Accessibility failure mode.** A boss/critical sequence may pass a normal visual review while using red strobe, movement, or low-contrast information that a player cannot use. **Test:** mandatory largest-scale flash analysis plus high-contrast/reduced-motion/muted clips; recruit player validation beyond color simulation before declaring the targets met.
- **Fairness/readability failure mode.** The density governor could cull a threat-adjacent visual differently by device frame rate, making one device appear easier. **Test:** record event priorities and cull decisions from the same deterministic replay at 30/60/120 render rates; assert rank-1/2 semantic artifacts and their positions/identities match, while frame interpolation may differ.
- **Reproducibility failure mode.** Wall-clock-seeded cosmetic variance or nondeterministic GPU order can make captures incomparable. **Test:** same replay, baseline-v1 manifest, viewport, settings, and asset manifest yields identical event/manifest/cull logs; capture exact ticks 0, 60, 120, 300, and 600 after the fixture begins. In 8-bit RGBA comparison, **Target:** <=0.5% of viewport pixels may differ by more than 3/255 in any RGB channel and **Target:** 0 such differing pixels may occur inside rank-1/2 semantic masks. Any random source must be derived from the observed event ID. Different renderers receive separate golden baselines and may not be compared as pixel-identical.
- **Constraint tension.** S1 favors redundant cues, while duplicating every automatic hit would overwhelm a mobile playfield. Resolution: redundancy is mandatory for rank-1/2 gameplay meaning; rank-4 texture is deliberately aggregated and may be absent.

## Experiments and telemetry

1. **Three-density comprehension study.** Run low, normal, and saturated deterministic fixtures in standard and reduced-motion modes. Capture correct identification of boss direction, skill area, crit/normal class, player/Gate health, and cooldown readiness; record confidence and “could not see it” reports. Gate against the event-table targets.
2. **Effect-budget staircase.** Increase rank-4 emitters in fixed increments while holding the same simulation replay. Measure rank-1 protected-zone intersection, target recognition, frame time, and subjective clutter. Stop at the first failed gate; the previous passing budget is the candidate cap.
3. **Contrast and sensory matrix.** Present clips in standard, high-contrast, reduced-motion, effects-muted, no-haptic, and combinations. Log which channel was available, but never treat optional haptics/audio as required success conditions.
4. **Replay authority audit.** Persist only local, privacy-minimal aggregate counters—event-kind count, cull count/reason, mask violation count, capture settings, and event-hash comparison—not player identity or network data. Inspect any source-event/presentation mismatch as a defect.
5. **Stage/boss interruption test.** Trigger stage entry, boss arrival, damage, and skill resolution in overlapping orders. **Target:** rank-1 notices preempt informative placards; movement and automatic combat remain live; recognition and input-continuity gates still pass.

## Decision

Make combat spectacle a bounded observer: stable survival HUD first, world-local confirmed consequences second, restrained texture last. The static presentation is the semantic contract; motion, audio, and haptic are optional enrichment. That keeps Abyssal Command readable on a small screen, safe under reduced-motion constraints, and faithful to deterministic movement-first automatic combat.

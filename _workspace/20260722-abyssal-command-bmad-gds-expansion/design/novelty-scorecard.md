# Novelty scorecard — variation that preserves purpose

**Stage 1 status:** a falsifiable scoring plan, not an entertainment claim. Every threshold is an **UNVERIFIED TARGET** and every gate is **UNPASSED** until fixed corpora and participant evidence are produced.

## What counts as novelty

Novelty is a changed player-readable decision under the same authored stage promise. A different background, particle color, raw seed, or unmeasured wave name is not sufficient. Conversely, variety fails if it removes an objective route, violates a pressure envelope, makes a stage unrecognizable, or obscures an essential cue.

## Scorecard

| Dimension | Qualifying variation | Quality floor | Metric / **UNVERIFIED TARGET** | Future evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Map route | Approved module/transform, detour, rejoin, route cost, landmark placement. | Fixed backbone, exactly one objective and Gate, valid primary path. | 100-key corpus: 100% valid; exact variable-subgraph collisions <=2%; one module <=40% of eligible placements when three or more alternatives exist. | Versioned `MapKey`/`MapPlan`, digest, validator, fingerprint report. |
| Objective readability | Different valid objective bearing/landmark inside a stable objective contract. | Objective and Gate roles are explicit before pressure commitment. | 12-person stage study: >=10/12 identify objective direction and Gate route; same threshold with reduced motion. | Static/normal captures and objective probes. |

## Anti-boredom decision rules

1. **Precompute, then play.** A stage seed selects from finite authored maps and encounter cards before the run. No run observes player frame time, audio latency, or presentation preference to seek novelty.
2. **Change one decision dimension at a time.** A pressure card may change lane/role/hazard composition, but it cannot simultaneously change the canonical objective, Gate order, reward rule, and stage result.
3. **Protect recognition before scoring variety.** Invalid maps, unreadable objective direction, inaccessible relief, or unsafe density score zero even if entropy/distance is high.
4. **Use relief as contrast, not downtime theater.** Relief must preserve a reachable path and current core loop; it must not pause the simulation, conceal an attack, auto-resolve an objective, or trigger a commerce/return prompt.
5. **Prevent repetition across layers.** The encounter-card history prevents short-pattern recurrence; narrative uses authored cooldown/history windows; audio variants derive from event ID; rank-4 hit texture aggregates. These histories are presentation/content selection tools and never rule inputs.
6. **Keep Domain recognizable.** W-04 carries the authored stage vocabulary across seeds. W-01 offers orientation, W-02 confirms the objective relationship, W-03 anchors risk, and W-05 records only result—not new fiction.

## Measurement interpretation

- Descriptor coverage, entropy, collision rate, n-gram frequency, and relief counts measure generated exposure. They do **not** prove boredom or fun.
- Boredom/repetition needs preregistered, local moderated self-report and must report skipped answers/missingness.
- A positive novelty measure cannot erase a failed map validity, readability, challenge-distribution, accessibility, privacy, or determinism check.
- A future score may compare grammar variants only when the descriptor schema, seed corpus, rule versions, challenge budget, and participant allocation are held constant.

## Stage 1 decision gates

| Decision | Pass condition | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Expand the map palette | Corpus meets validity/readability first, then route-variation targets. | **UNPASSED** |
| Expand encounter-card combinations | The 12-card anti-repetition and relief rules hold without route/challenge regression. | **UNPASSED** |
| Add a new presentation variant | It has a caption/static fallback, observer event ID, density classification, and replay-stable selection. | **UNPASSED** |
| Add a permanent progression surface | It remains disclosed, finite, active-confirmed, equal-power, and passes parity/idle checks. | **UNPASSED** |
| Call the loop less repetitive | Preregistered exposure measures plus participant self-report meet their study criteria. | **UNPASSED** |

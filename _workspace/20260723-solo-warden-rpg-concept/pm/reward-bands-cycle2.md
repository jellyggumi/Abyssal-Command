## Axis 1 — Comeback Mechanics (≤30% reversal reinterpreted for PvE): cross-archetype resource-starvation check

**Question**: is there any point in the 10-stage progression where a `casual`-archetype player (lowest investment discipline — 30% skip-rate on investment decisions per `qa/gate-measurements.md#g3`) becomes mathematically unable to catch up to a `micro-optimizer`-tier player within the same campaign?

**Measured**: `echoCoreEarned` and `wardLevel` were read from every `stageResults` entry across all 21 runs (7 archetypes × 3 seeds) — not just the final stage, every one of the 10 checkpoints per run (210 data points total for each field).

- **Every single stage checkpoint, across all 7 archetypes and all 3 seeds, is byte-identical**: Stage 1 = `echoCoreEarned:4, wardLevel:1`; Stage 2 = `8,3`; Stage 3 = `12,4`; Stage 4 = `16,6`; Stage 5 = `20,7`; Stage 6 = `24,8`; Stage 7 = `28,9`; Stage 8 = `32,10`; Stage 9 = `36,12`; Stage 10 (final) = `40,13`. Zero divergence found at any of the 10 checkpoints across any archetype pairing (verified programmatically: `casual` vs `micro-optimizer` per-stage diff = 0 at all 10 points, seed 301; full 7-archetype × 3-seed cross-check confirms the same for all 21 runs).
- All 21 runs reach `terminal: "FINAL_COMPLETION"` with `outcome: "victory"` at every one of the 10 stages — 100% clear rate, no archetype fails or stalls anywhere in the progression (this reconfirms the Cycle 1/Cycle 2 finding already logged at `qa/gate-measurements.md#g3`).
- **Why this happens (mechanical cause, confirmed against `design/stage-progression.md` §1)**: Echo Core and Ward Level are boss-clear-driven, not archetype-choice-driven. Per `stage-progression.md`, elite-extraction grants `+1` Echo Core and boss-clear grants `+3` (net `+4`/stage, 10 stages × 4 = 40 total budget), and `wardLevel = resolvedIds.length + floor(companionCollection.length/2)` — both are functions of *stages cleared*, not of stat/trait/loadout investment choices. Since all 7 archetypes clear all 10 stages on the first attempt every seed, the resource curve that feeds Echo Core/Ward Level is identical by construction, regardless of playstyle.

**Verdict: MET.** There is no cross-archetype resource-starvation gap anywhere in the 10-stage progression — a `casual`-discipline player's Echo Core/Ward Level trajectory is mathematically identical to a `micro-optimizer`'s at every single checkpoint, not just convergent by the end. This is a stronger result than the original 30%-reversal-threshold question asks for: there is no gap to reverse in the first place, because the resource axis this axis interrogates (Echo Core/Ward Level) doesn't respond to playstyle at all — it responds only to stage-clear count, which is uniform across all measured archetypes. The actual skill-differentiated axis (in-combat power, TTK) is a separate, already-measured question (`qa/gate-measurements.md#g2`'s per-archetype TTK spread, 0.81×-1.166× vs median) — and the `pm/lane-reward-bands.md` §1 "Formation Surge" mechanic (formerly "Full Rally") exists precisely to smooth *that* axis, not the resource axis this check confirms is already flat. This axis's finding is a precondition-check in favor of that design: because resources never diverge, Formation Surge only ever needs to correct in-combat power gaps, not resource-accumulation gaps — a simpler, single-axis comeback problem than the original commerce-shaped template assumed.

## Axis 2 — Playstyle-Parity Session Bands (10-20 reinterpretation): completionist-collector `[20,40]` band vs measured campaign length

**Question**: `qa/lane-archetype-testplan.md` §4.6 (`completionist-collector`, "Full Roster") proposes `sessions_to_full_roster_completion: band: [20, 40]`, `band_status: TARGET_PENDING`, sourced as "harness 기본 10-20 세션 패리티를 완주 축으로 확장한 QA 초기 가설" (an extrapolation of the harness default 10-20 session parity band onto the completion axis, awaiting this PM lane's confirmation per that file's `band_source` note). Does the campaign as currently scoped reach `[20,40]` sessions within a single 10-stage campaign?

**Measured** (`qa/evidence-cycle2/sweep-completionist-collector.json`, all 3 seeds):

- Every seed (301/302/303) produces exactly **10** `stageResults` entries, terminating at `gate-zenith` with `terminal: "FINAL_COMPLETION"`. Under this project's own session-unit convention (1 stage-clear ≈ 1 session, the same unit `qa/lane-archetype-testplan.md` uses for its band), **the full campaign is 10 sessions long, identically across all 3 seeds** — there is no seed-to-seed variance in campaign length to average.
- Full-roster tracking (loadout composition, seed 301): roster grows `1→2→3→4(new roster member)→4→4→4→4→5(new)→5` companions in the loadout field across stages 1-10, and cross-referencing `design/stage-progression.md` §1's cumulative-roster column confirms the same curve project-wide (`1,2,3,4,5,5,5,5,6,6` — full 6-companion roster reached at **Stage 9**, one stage before campaign end). Trait unlocks (seed 301) accumulate `0→1→1→2→2→3→3→4→4→5` across the 10 stages, at the unlock checkpoints `design/stage-progression.md` §1 marks (stages 2/4/6/8/10) — 5 trait picks total by campaign end, each a 1-of-3 branch choice (`qa/lane-archetype-testplan.md` §4.6: "트레잇 다양성/완주를 추구").
- **10 < 20** (the band's own floor). A single completionist-collector campaign run cannot reach the `[20,40]` session band — it terminates at session 10, less than half the floor value, regardless of seed.

**Why the gap exists (not a defect — a scope mismatch)**: the QA band was extrapolated from the harness's generic paid/free-parity default (`pm/lane-reward-bands.md` §0's cited original template, `steady.parity_sessions_band: [10,20]`, doubled to `[20,40]` for the stricter "full completion" bar) without accounting for this project's fixed single-campaign length. The band implicitly assumes repeatable session accumulation past one campaign's end — but `design/UNIFIED-GDD.md` §12 item 6 states plainly: **"Bound Fragment 재도전/NG+ 가용성 — 현재 '보스 1회 처치=캠페인 전체 1회'만 확정 커밋, 재도전 시 재계산 필요"** (New-Game-Plus/repeat-campaign availability is undecided; only "one boss-clear = one full campaign" is committed, and any repeat-run scope needs separate recalculation). Item 6 is listed among the Stage 2-required unresolved issues, not yet closed. Full roster (6/6) is in fact reachable *inside* one campaign, by Stage 9 — well before the band's [20,40] floor — but full *trait*-branch coverage (5 unlock points × 3 choices each = far more combinations than one campaign's 5 sequential picks can exhaust) structurally requires replaying the campaign, i.e. NG+. `[20,40]` sessions maps to roughly 2-4 campaign repeats at 10 sessions/campaign — a plausible NG+-scoped target [INFERENCE, not sourced from any document — offered as a sanity-check on the band's shape, not a confirmed design decision] — but nothing in this cycle's artifacts commits to that mapping, because NG+ itself is undecided.

**Verdict: N/A-out-of-scope-this-campaign-structure.** The `[20,40]` band cannot be evaluated as MET or NOT MET against the current single-campaign structure, because the campaign structure that would let a player accumulate 20+ sessions toward completionist goals (repeat/NG+ campaigns) does not yet exist as a committed design decision (`UNIFIED-GDD.md` §12 item 6, open). What *is* measured and confirmed: one full campaign = 10 sessions exactly (all 3 seeds), full 6-companion roster reachable within that single campaign by session 9, but full 5-trait-branch coverage is not — the QA band's premise (multi-campaign accumulation) is real, but gated on a decision this cycle didn't make. Recommend to QA: either rescope the band's floor down to what's reachable in one campaign (session ≤10, if "full roster" is the actual bar rather than "full trait-branch coverage"), or explicitly mark it NG+-scoped and re-open once item 6 is resolved.

## Axis 3 — Resource-Yield Fairness (economy-greed archetype): PRED-04 check

**Question**: `qa/lane-archetype-testplan.md` §5 `PRED-04` flags a risk that `economy-greed`'s elite-farming-optimized behavior could produce resource yield that outpaces the intended campaign pacing (severity S1, "경계 위반 가능성"), with `primary_metric: resource_yield_per_session_minute` (`qa/lane-archetype-testplan.md` §4.3). Since Echo Core is stated to come from boss-clear (not archetype choice), does `economy-greed` actually get measurably more?

**Measured, per-stage Echo Core yield (`sweep-economy-greed.json` vs the other 6 archetype sweep files, all 3 seeds)**:

- Per-stage `echoCoreEarned` delta sequence is **`[4,4,4,4,4,4,4,4,4,4]`, totaling 40, identically for all 7 archetypes across all 3 seeds** (21/21 runs match). `economy-greed`'s cumulative Echo Core trajectory is byte-identical to every other archetype at every one of the 10 checkpoints — this reconfirms Axis 1's finding from the yield-rate angle: Echo Core is structurally boss-clear-gated, and `economy-greed`'s farming-optimized formation/stat choices don't touch that gate.
- The testplan's actual primary metric is **rate**, not raw total — `resource_yield_per_session_minute`. Computed from `ticksUsed` (session real-time cost) at `TICK_RATE=60`, echoCore-per-minute across the full 10-stage campaign, averaged over 3 seeds:

| archetype | seed 301 | seed 302 | seed 303 | avg (echoCore/min) | rank (1=fastest yield) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| rusher | 5.730 | 5.667 | 5.726 | **5.708** | 1 |
| micro-optimizer | 5.598 | 5.449 | 5.435 | 5.494 | 2 |
| casual | 5.448 | 5.373 | 5.525 | 5.449 | 3 |
| completionist-collector | 5.279 | 5.291 | 5.234 | 5.268 | 4 |
| turtle | 5.048 | 5.100 | 5.062 | 5.070 | 5 |
| **economy-greed** | **4.977** | **5.040** | **5.115** | **5.044** | **6** |
| single-companion-main | 4.846 | 4.829 | 4.866 | 4.847 | 7 |

- `economy-greed` average yield rate: **5.044 echoCore/min**. Mean of the other 6 archetypes: **5.306 echoCore/min**. Delta: **-0.262 (-4.94%)** — `economy-greed` yields *less* per minute than the cross-archetype average, ranking 6th of 7 (only `single-companion-main` is slower). This is the opposite of PRED-04's predicted direction (outpacing intended pacing); the mechanical reason is that `economy-greed`'s stat/formation priorities (`qa/lane-archetype-testplan.md` §4.3: "생존+처치속도의 균형", not burst damage) trade combat speed for durability, which costs session-time (`ticksUsed`) without any compensating Echo Core bonus — since Echo Core is flat-rate per boss-clear regardless of how long the clear took.

**Scope limitation (explicit)**: this dataset is 21 single-attempt, full-10-stage campaign sweeps (`terminal: "FINAL_COMPLETION"` every run, no retries recorded in any `stageResults` array). It measures whether `economy-greed`'s stat/formation choices yield disproportionate Echo Core within one standard campaign traversal — they do not. It does **not** exercise the specific mechanism PRED-04's S1-severity clause actually warns about: a repeated elite-farming *loop* (hunt→extract→materialize→capture repeated without progressing to assault) that might bypass the "no-background-combat / session당 단일 결정론적 정산" boundary (`qa/lane-archetype-testplan.md` §4.3 `boundary_check`). No such loop exists in this sweep's data — each run is a linear 10-stage clear — so that specific boundary-violation risk remains unverified by this evidence set and is not resolved by this axis's MET verdict below.

**Verdict: MET** (for the yield-rate-per-stage-traversal question this axis's data can answer). `economy-greed` produces **no** measurable Echo Core advantage over the other 6 archetypes — per-stage yield is identical (structural, boss-clear-gated) and per-minute yield rate is 4.94% *below* the 6-archetype mean, not above it. PRED-04's specific predicted exploit (resource yield outpacing intended pacing) is not observed in this dataset. The narrower farming-loop boundary-violation risk PRED-04 also names is **out of scope for this evidence set** (no repeated-loop runs exist in `qa/evidence-cycle2/`) and should remain open on `qa/exploit-register.md` (or equivalent) pending a dedicated farming-loop test, not closed by this MET verdict.

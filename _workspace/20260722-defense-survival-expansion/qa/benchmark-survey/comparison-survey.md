# Defense-survivor comparison survey

**Survey date:** 2026-07-22  
**Scope:** five named comparables; official developer, publisher, and platform-store descriptions only.  
**Question:** which proven patterns should calibrate the mobile/public beat, and how often do surveyed descriptions evidence the complete Echo-command extraction hook?

## Method and evidence limits

This is a desk survey, not hands-on testing of the comparables and not a human impression panel. “Not evidenced” means only that the complete mechanic was not found in the surveyed official descriptions; it is not proof of absence from every version, mode, or update. Readability and public-beat recommendations are analyst calibrations derived from cited facts. No comparable score is treated as an Abyssal Surge playtest result.

The candidate hook is evaluated only as the conjunction: **defeat an elite → make a bounded extraction decision → obtain a named permanent companion → frame that companion as recovered enemy memory**. A partial match does not count.

## Source-backed comparable profiles

### Vampire Survivors

The official Steam description calls it a minimal-input time-survival roguelite, says gold gathered in each run buys upgrades for the next survivor, and lists mouse, keyboard, controller, and touch-screen support. Its tips emphasize collecting persistent gems/items and choosing then leveling offensive weapons. This is the cleanest control-load reference for auto-combat movement and mobile pickup readability. The surveyed description does not evidence lanes, an occupation point, or the complete Echo-command extraction conjunction.

### Brotato

The official Steam description specifies auto-firing by default with optional manual aim, waves lasting 20–90 seconds, sub-30-minute runs, material collection for experience, and between-wave item shopping. It also advertises difficulty controls for enemy health, damage, and speed. This is the strongest reference for short, repeatable pressure windows and explicit tuning knobs. The surveyed descriptions do not evidence persistent conversion of defeated elites into named recovered-memory allies.

### Arknights

The official site identifies Arknights as a strategic RPG mobile game; its official metadata identifies tower defense, deployment, and skill activation. That establishes it as the lane-defense/mobile tactics reference without relying on player reviews. Its control grammar is placement and timed activation rather than continuous avatar movement, so Abyssal Surge should borrow lane/chokepath legibility, not its interaction density. The surveyed official description does not evidence the complete Echo-command extraction conjunction.

### Hades

The official Steam description states that each escape attempt grows the player stronger and advances the story, offers dozens of Boons and thousands of viable builds, provides a fully voiced cast and thousands of story events, and uses the Mirror of Night for permanent growth. It explicitly highlights hand-painted environments and an original score. Hades is therefore the presentation, authored-transition, and long-term narrative-progression ceiling—not an auto-combat or lane-defense control reference. Befriending gods, ghosts, and monsters is not evidence that a defeated elite is extracted as a permanent recovered-memory combat companion.

### Death Must Die

The official Steam description calls it a roguelite RPG bullet heaven, documents hundreds of synergistic blessings, randomized unique items that ease later attempts, relic-risk escalation, varied heroes, bosses/Guardians, shrines, and personal hero stories. It is the closest surveyed build-synergy and 2.5D/isometric action reference. Its surveyed PC store page does not establish mobile touch support, lane occupation, or the complete Echo-command extraction conjunction.

## Public-beat calibration for Abyssal Surge

1. **Mobile input:** match Vampire Survivors’ low simultaneous-input burden while retaining a 44px minimum for explicit extraction, growth, and reward decisions. Continuous movement plus auto-fire should remain the default; optional actions must never require multi-touch during pressure.
2. **Wave grammar:** Brotato’s documented 20–90 second waves validate short pressure units. The authoritative Abyssal loop should expose scout → pressure → flank → ranged → elite → boss beats inside the stage rather than one undifferentiated stream.
3. **Defense readability:** use Arknights as the lane/chokepath reference. Each of ten stages needs visually distinct routes and an immediately readable threatened path; elevation, hazards, occupation points, and flanks must affect movement, range, or recovery rather than remain decorative.
4. **Growth separation:** keep run items, three-choice skills, stat changes, synergies, extracted companions, stage rewards, and Archive growth visibly separate. Hades and Death Must Die show the value of run-to-run progression and build identity; Brotato shows the value of bounded between-wave choice.
5. **Presentation:** target Hades-like authored transitions and audio identity only as a quality direction, not as a scope promise. Renderer/audio must observe deterministic simulation events; they must not author outcomes.
6. **2.5D clarity:** compare every effect against four fixed questions: can the player locate the gate, priority threat, occupation/extraction point, and safe movement route in under three seconds?

**Measured comparable frequency:** `0 / 5` in this bounded official-description survey; threshold is `≤2 / 5`.  
**Finding:** Echo-command extraction remains a **supported novelty candidate** because the complete conjunction was not evidenced in any of the five surveyed descriptions.  
**Gate status:** **FIX**, not PASS. The frequency arm is supported, but the required human impression score (target ≥4/5) has not been collected. Automated sessions and analyst inference cannot substitute for five human impressions.

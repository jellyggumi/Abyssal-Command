# Benchmark survey research plan

## Main question
How does Abyssal Surge's current defense-survivor public beat compare with Vampire Survivors, Brotato, Arknights, Hades, and Death Must Die across mobile controls, auto-combat movement, lane defense, extraction, long-term progression, cutscenes, sound, and 2.5D combat readability—and is **Echo-command extraction** (a defeated elite becomes a named permanent companion represented as recovered enemy memory) uncommon enough to remain a supported novelty candidate?

## Subtopics
1. **Survivors controls and run structure:** verify Vampire Survivors, Brotato, and Death Must Die from official/store/developer sources, focusing on movement/auto-combat, mobile/controller support, run rewards, progression, extraction-adjacent mechanics, audiovisual readability, and narrative presentation.
2. **Defense and lane readability:** verify Arknights from official/store/developer sources, focusing on mobile controls, lane/placement defense, operator growth, audiovisual telegraphing, story/cutscenes, and whether defeated enemies become permanent named allies/memories.
3. **Action-RPG progression and presentation:** verify Hades from official/store/developer sources, focusing on controls, room exits/extraction analogy, persistent progression, story/cutscenes, audio, isometric/2.5D readability, and whether defeated enemies become permanent named allies/memories.
4. **Cross-title synthesis:** normalize claims into one comparison matrix, explicitly mark unknowns/absence claims, distinguish sourced fact from analyst calibration, and calculate comparable frequency for the candidate hook.

## Source standard
Prefer official game sites, developer/publisher pages, platform store pages, and official support/manual pages. Secondary sources may clarify an otherwise undocumented mechanic but must not substitute for available primary sources. An absence claim is phrased as “not evidenced in surveyed official descriptions,” never proof that no such feature exists.

## Synthesis output
The final survey will live at `qa/benchmark-survey/comparison-survey.md`. It will include dated scope/method, source ledger with URLs and access date, an eight-axis comparison matrix, mobile/public-beat calibration, and a bounded G8 novelty finding. No automated observation will be labeled a human impression or panel.
